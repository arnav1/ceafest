You can’t build a great building on a weak foundation.

You must have a solid foundation if you are going to have a strong superstructure.

Foundation is the base of any structure. Without a firm foundation, the structure cannot stand. The strength of the foundation determines the life of structure. The design of foundation depends on soil and load. There has been a surge of infrastructural development in India during the last few years. High rise buildings have become nations’ symbol of pride and development. The foundation design is what matters. Hence it is a big challenge for us civil engineers to design a foundation for a high rise building in Indian conditions.

The participants are required to design a foundation for a high rise building, ensuring that it can stand the test of time.

It should be noted that the foundation design should not only bear the load of the building but also be cost efficient.

The participating teams should make a presentation giving their analysis, approach and factors considered in designing the foundation, along with the technical details of the foundation. The participants are required to devise innovative techniques to build the foundation of a building with given specifications.

Concrete is as we know the heart of modern day construction. For a commoner, civil engineering means concrete. Its unique features make it the best choice as a material. Many people think all concrete is the same. This is not true! Concrete is no longer a simple grey mass, but it has made its way to become pervious (lighter with honey comb structures), more beautiful (architectural concrete), colored, stronger (high strength concrete), self-compacting to name a few.

Today in India, we see that in cities, storm-water runoff is a big challenge during monsoon. Storm water runoff increases both erosion and flooding influencing both water quality and quantity problems. A simple solution to avoiding these problems is to stop installing the impervious surfaces that block natural water infiltration into the soil. Rather than building them with conventional concrete or asphalt, more and more communities, municipalities, and businesses are switching to pervious concrete or porous pavement, a material that offers the inherent durability and low life-cycle costs of a typical concrete pavement while retaining storm water runoff and replenishing local watershed systems. Already one of the most happening topics in Research & Development, pervious concrete is rapidly gaining popularity in the industrial sector as well. CEA Fest’s Concrete Challenge offers the right platform to solve this problem from this aspect.

Concrete challenge returns this year with a problem statement that demands participants to design such pervious concrete mix which satisfies required strength and permeability requirements as well as is lightweight because of the abundance of pores. Achieving this through thorough research and presenting it to win the judges over is the ‘challenge’ Concrete Challenge has to offer you.

To make two 100x150 mm (diameter 100mm, length 150mm) pervious concrete cylinders having

  • High permeability
  • Good compressive strength
  • Minimal Density
Both the cylinders should be made out of same material and should satisfy all above conditions. The cylinders should be cast 7 days prior to the event.

The Earth’s ecosystems are now at a critical stage due to urbanization. Buildings and construction works have the largest single share in global resource use and pollution emission.

“What is the use of a house if you don’t have a decent planet to put it on?”

Sustainable designs are scientific attempts in order to protect the gifted planet.
